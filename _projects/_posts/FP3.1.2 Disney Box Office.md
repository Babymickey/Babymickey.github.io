### Evolution of Disney
Since the release of Snow White in 1937, Disney has transitioned into a media powerhouse. This transition is clearly visible when looking at Disney Movie Box Office data over the last 30 years. In the beginning, Disney releases were sporadic, then there was a boom in movies during the Disney Renaissance Era. As Disney found its formula for movies and acquired big-name franchises such as “Avengers”, it has reached unprecedented heights. This interactive chart allows you to explore every major release and filter by genre to see how Disney’s output has increased in the 21st century.

### Strategy by Genre: What makes the most?
When it's broken down by genre, you may begin to pick up on Disney’s strategy. While “Musical”, “Drama” and “Comedy” defined the studio's early identity and remain high-performing categories, “Adventure”, “Action” and “Drama” have become the primary drivers of Disney in the modern era. This shift suggests that Disney has moved toward elaborate, broad-appeal movies that can perform well in the international market as well. Looking at the average gross per genre in the chart below makes it clear why the studio focuses on adventure sagas.

### The Impact of the Renaissance Era?
To understand Disney’s modern dominance, it's important to look back at the “Renaissance Era”, between 1989 and 1999. After stagnation in the 1980s, this decade saw a string of massive hits, starting with “The Little Mermaid” and peaking at “The Lion King”, which revitalized the Disney brand. The chart below highlights the top performers from the Renaissance era, showcasing how Disney reclaimed the box office and set the stage for their current height. These films developed the nostalgia of Disney, which allowed Disney to leverage this for the current live-action remakes.
